Understanding what makes a hurricane strong is how we forecast and prepare for them. Seed the Storm turns that science into a game: read the real atmosphere and pick the seed that will intensify the most.

How to play

  1. Pick a basin below, then play 6 rounds — one per in-season month (each from a random year). Watch the shear and ocean warmth shift month to month on the real ERA5 environment. Random NH deals a different basin every round.
  2. Four candidate TC seeds drop onto the map (the colored pins).
  3. Pick the seed you think will spin up into the storm with the most Accumulated Cyclone Energy (ACE) — a measure of a storm's total power over its whole life, so a long-lived hurricane scores more than a brief intense one.
  4. We integrate every seed forward through the real winds, shear, and ocean until it dissipates — then reveal who was right and score you against the best possible pick.

Warm water + low shear + a long track over open ocean = big ACE. Land and shear pull storms apart. ▶ Watch how it works
